## Task 1: Initial Mechanical Design for a Simple Robotic Dog

**Date:** 2026-07-10

**Track:** Mechanical

**Status:** In Progress

**Objective:**  
Create an initial mechanical design for a simple robotic dog and understand the basic mechanical principles that allow the robot to stand and walk.

**Tools / Software Used:**  
To be added.

**Task Description:**  
This task focuses on creating the first mechanical design concept for a simple robotic dog. The goal is not to build a highly advanced robotic dog, but to understand the mechanical fundamentals that allow a walking robot to stand, balance, and move.

The design should focus on the following points:

- Body and frame shape
- Leg design
- Number of joints and degrees of freedom
- Motor selection
- Basic torque calculation for one joint
- Possible degrees of freedom
- Suggested walking method
- Expected mechanical problems

**Steps:**  
1. Study the general body structure of a robotic dog.
2. Choose a simple shape for the robot body and frame.
3. Design the basic leg structure.
4. Decide how many joints each leg should have.
5. Determine the required degrees of freedom for standing and walking.
6. Select suitable motors for the joints.
7. Calculate the basic torque required for at least one joint.
8. Suggest a simple walking method for the robot.
9. Identify the expected mechanical problems that may appear during design or movement.

**Result / Output:**  
An initial mechanical design concept for a simple robotic dog will be created. The design should explain the robot body, leg structure, joints, motor selection, basic torque calculation, walking method, and expected mechanical limitations.

**Challenges:**  
The expected challenges include choosing the correct number of joints, keeping the robot stable, selecting suitable motors, calculating torque correctly, and making sure the robot can balance while standing and walking.

**What I Learned:**  
This task helps me understand that robotic movement depends heavily on mechanical design, not only programming or electronics. I learned that body shape, leg structure, joint placement, torque, balance, and degrees of freedom are important factors when designing a walking robot.
